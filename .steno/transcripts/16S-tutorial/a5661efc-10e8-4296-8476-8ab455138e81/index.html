Based on my research, here's a comprehensive overview of pediatric celiac disease microbiome papers by Dr. Maureen Leonard and collaborators:

Key Papers from the CDGEMM Study

1. Microbiome Signatures Before Celiac Disease Onset (2021) ⭐

Citation: Leonard MM, Valitutti F, Karathia H, et al. Microbiome signatures of progression toward celiac disease onset in at-risk children in a longitudinal prospective cohort study. Proc Natl Acad Sci USA. 2021;118(29):e2020322118.

Dataset Access: - BioProject ID: PRJNA486782 - SRA Accession: SRP158417 - Available at: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/bioproject/PRJNA486782

Study Design: - 20 children (10 cases, 10 controls) - 118 fecal samples across 7 timepoints - Longitudinal sampling every 3 months for 18 months before CD onset - Sequencing: Shotgun metagenomic sequencing (Illumina HiSeq 4000, 2×150bp) - Platform: CosmosID for taxonomic profiling (strain-level resolution)

Key Findings: - Microbiome changes detected 18 months before disease onset - Increased proinflammatory species: Dialister invisus, Parabacteroides sp. - Decreased protective species: Faecalibacterium prausnitzii, Streptococcus thermophilus - Altered tryptophan metabolism and serine/threonine metabolites

2. IgA Response and Plasma Metabolome Dynamics (2023) ⭐

Citation: Girdhar K, Dogru YD, Huang Q, et al. Dynamics of the gut microbiome, IgA response, and plasma metabolome in the development of pediatric celiac disease. Microbiome. 2023;11:9.

Dataset Access: - BioProject ID: PRJNA631001 - GitHub code: https://github.com/altindislab/celiac-gut-microbiome

Study Design: - ABIS cohort (All Babies in Southeast Sweden) - 16-31 children per timepoint - Samples at ages 2.5 and 5 years - Sequencing: 16S rRNA (V4 region, Illumina MiSeq) - Novel approach: IgA-sequencing combining bacterial sorting with 16S

Key Findings: - 2-fold increase in IgA-coated bacteria in progressors - 117 differentially abundant ASVs at age 2.5; 71 at age 5 - TDCA (taurodeoxycholic acid) elevated 2-fold in progressors - TDCA feeding caused villous atrophy in mouse models - Distinct inflammatory cytokine profile (IFN-α2, IL-1α, IL-17E)

Note: Dr. Maureen Leonard is not listed as an author on this paper, but it's from the same research group/cohort.

3. Zonulin as a Biomarker (2024)

Citation: Leonard MM, et al. Zonulin as a Biomarker for the Development of Celiac Disease. Pediatrics. 2024;153(1):e2023063050.

Study Design: - 102 children from CDGEMM study (2014-2022) - 51 CDA cases + matched controls - Serum zonulin measured from 12 months to CDA onset

Key Findings: - Zonulin levels rise 18.3 months (range 6-78) before diagnosis - Antibiotic exposure associated with increased zonulin - Potential for preclinical screening in at-risk children - Links intestinal permeability to disease development

4. Metabolic Pathways Predict Disease Years Before Diagnosis (2025) ⭐

Citation: Kelley K, Dogru D, Huang Q, et al. Children who develop celiac disease are predicted to exhibit distinct metabolic pathways among their gut microbiota years before diagnosis. Microbiol Spectr. 2025;13(3):e0146824.

Study Design: - Fecal samples from first 5 years of life - Three phases of microbiome development analyzed - Functional pathway analysis

Key Findings: - Enriched microbial pathways at age 1 predict disease - Pathways linked to bacterial pathogenesis and inflammation - Key taxa: Lachnospiraceae, Alistipes, Bifidobacterium dentium - Potential for early intervention strategies

Note: Dr. Maureen Leonard is not listed as an author, but builds on CDGEMM work.

About the CDGEMM Study

Full Name: Celiac Disease Genomic, Environmental, Microbiome, and Metabolomic Study

Study Characteristics: - International prospective birth cohort (US, Italy, Spain) - 500 genetically at-risk infants followed from birth to age 10 - 36,500+ biospecimens collected since 2014 - 145,500+ clinical data points - Multi-omic approach: genomics, microbiome, metabolomics, environmental factors

Principal Investigator: Dr. Maureen M. Leonard, MD, MMSc - Clinical Director, Center for Celiac Research and Treatment, MGH - Assistant Professor of Pediatrics, Harvard Medical School - 83 publications, 1,582 citations

Based on my research, none of these celiac disease datasets have ready-to-use phyloseq objects available in aggregated databases like Qiita or CuratedMetagenomicData. However, there are several ways to access and work with the data:

Current Data Availability Status

❌ NOT Available As:

  • Pre-processed phyloseq RDS objects
  • Qiita database entries (I couldn't find these studies in Qiita)
  • CuratedMetagenomicData packages
  • MGnify processed datasets

✅ Available As:

1. Raw Sequencing Data (NCBI SRA)

Leonard et al. 2021 PNAS Study: - BioProject: PRJNA486782 - SRA: SRP158417 - Format: Shotgun metagenomic sequencing (Illumina HiSeq 4000, 2×150bp) - Samples: 118 fecal samples

Girdhar et al. 2023 Microbiome Study: - BioProject: PRJNA631001 - Format: 16S rRNA (V4 region, MiSeq) + IgA-seq - GitHub: https://github.com/altindislab/celiac-gut-microbiome (code only, no data files)

2. Supplementary Data Tables (Excel/CSV)

The PNAS 2021 paper has downloadable supplementary datasets: - Dataset S1-S6 available at: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2020322118 - Direct download example: https://www.pnas.org/content/pnas/suppl/2021/07/08/2020322118.DCSupplemental/pnas.2020322118.sd02.xlsx

These contain: - Taxonomic profiling (species/strain level) - Functional pathway annotations - Metabolite measurements - Metadata

3. Analysis Code (GitHub)

Two repositories with analysis pipelines: - altindislab/celiac-gut-microbiome - Minimal documentation, R scripts only - PMilletich/Celiac_Disease_Pipeline - 7-step pipeline with PIME analysis
